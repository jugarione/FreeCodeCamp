Here's a time line of Elon's life:

  • 1971 - Musk was born on June 28
  • 1981 - The first computer: At age 10, he developed an intenerst in computing with the Commodore VIC-20
  • 1983 - The first programm: He taught himselg computer programming at the age of 12, sold the code of a BASIC-based video game he created called Blastar, to a magazine called PC and Office Technology, for approximately $500.
  • 1997 - Found Zip2: Musk and his brother started Zip2, a web software company.
  • 1999 - Found X.COM: Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company, with US$10 million from the sale of Zip2.
  • 2001 - Project of "Mars Oasis": Musk conceptualized "Mars Oasis"; a project to land a miniature experimental greenhouse on Mars, containing food crops growing on Martian regolith, in an attempt to regain public interest in space exploration.
  • 2001 - Found PayPal: One year later, the company merged with Confinity, which had a money transfer service called PayPal. The merged company focused on the PayPal service and was renamed PayPal
  • 2003 - Found SpaceX: With US$100 million of his early fortune, Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies, or SpaceX.
  • 2004 - Found Tesla: Musk led the Series A round of investment , joining Tesla's board of directors as its chairman. Musk took an active role within the company and oversaw Roadster product design at a detailed level, but was not deeply involved in day-to-day business operations.
  • 2011 - Contract with NASA: US$1.6 billion NASA Commercial Resupply Services program contract on December 23, 2008, for 12 flights of its Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon spacecraft to the Space Station, replacing the US Space Shuttle after it retired
  • 2015 - Found OpenAi: Musk announced the creation of OpenAI, a not-for-profit artificial intelligence (AI) research company.
  • 2018 - Falcon Heavy: The Falcon Heavy is a reusable super heavy-lift launch vehicle designed and manufactured by SpaceX.
  • 2020 - lorem ipsum: On May 30, 2020, SpaceX launched its first manned flight called Demo-2 becoming the first private company to both place a person into orbit and to eventually dock a manned space-craft with the ISS.
